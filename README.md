# Data-Visualization-Project-4

## Overview

This notebook presents a series of **data visualizations** created from an e-commerce dataset containing **1,200 transactions**. Using **Matplotlib** and **Seaborn**, the notebook transforms aggregated business data into clear graphical representations that highlight sales performance, marketing effectiveness, and temporal trends.

The visualizations complement the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) by making patterns, comparisons, and trends easier to interpret for business decision-making.

## Objectives

The notebook aims to:

* Visualize product sales performance.
* Compare revenue generated by different marketing channels.
* Analyze monthly revenue and transaction trends.
* Present business insights through clear and informative charts.

## Visualization Workflow

### 1. Product Performance Visualization

This visualization combines a **bar chart** and a **line chart** to analyze product performance.

The chart displays:

* Revenue generated by each product category.
* Total number of orders received for each product.

Using dual axes allows users to compare product revenue alongside order volume, helping identify products that generate high sales through either premium pricing or large transaction volumes.

### 2. Referral Source Revenue Analysis

A horizontal **bar chart** is used to compare revenue generated by different customer acquisition channels.

The visualization highlights:

* Revenue contribution from each referral source.
* Relative effectiveness of marketing channels.

This analysis helps determine which marketing platforms produce the greatest financial return and can guide future marketing investments.

### 3. Monthly Revenue and Order Trend Analysis

A dual-axis **line chart** illustrates business performance over time.

The visualization presents:

* Monthly total revenue.
* Monthly transaction volume.

The chart also includes annotations highlighting:

* Peak revenue periods.
* Months experiencing lower sales performance.

This visualization enables users to identify seasonal patterns, business growth, and fluctuations in customer purchasing activity.

## Libraries Used

* **Pandas** – Data preparation and aggregation.
* **Matplotlib** – Chart creation and customization.
* **Seaborn** – Enhanced statistical visualizations.

## Visualizations Included

The notebook generates the following figures:

* **Product Analysis:** Revenue vs. Order Volume
* **Referral Source Revenue Comparison**
* **Monthly Revenue and Transaction Trends**

Each visualization is also saved as a high-resolution PNG image for reporting and presentation purposes.

## Expected Outcomes

After executing the notebook, users will be able to:

* Compare the financial performance of different products.
* Evaluate the effectiveness of marketing referral sources.
* Observe monthly sales and transaction trends.
* Identify peak business periods and seasonal fluctuations.
* Support business decisions using graphical insights instead of raw numerical summaries.

## Conclusion

This data visualization notebook converts aggregated business data into meaningful graphical representations that improve data interpretation and communication. By combining bar charts and line charts, the notebook effectively illustrates product performance, marketing efficiency, and monthly business trends, providing valuable insights that support strategic planning and data-driven decision-making.
